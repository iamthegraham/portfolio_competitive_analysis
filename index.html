Portfolio Analysis- R. Graham Littlejohn

Sofa, five-box, accessed 1/16/2012 at http://www.madebysofa.com/archive/
Brand
A clean, minimalist, yet fun five-box design, Sofa's site feels right for the simple yet effective iconography they do. Despite being black text on a white background, Sofa avoids looking stuffy with the main image of lounge chairs sitting askew, living up to the "friendly" from their slogan. The picture of the cat with laser eyes reinforces that.
Interactivity
Not too much going on here- links change color and light up on mouseover as expected. One cool feature is in the "company" section, where staff photos turn from black-and-white to color on hover in order to highlight them.
Functionality
It was easy to find both examples of their work ("design" tab, obviously). Contact information abounds was under the "Company" tab; while I found it quickly, someone else might have spent too much time looking under another tab before finding it.
Navigation
The way the page drops down to move the navigation bar to the top instead of going to a new page is intuitive, as is the simpplicity of the layout. The boldness of the nav bar (utilizing the bottom four boxes of the five-box layout as navigation) eliminates the possibility of "banner blindness."

Nuemedia, advanced grid, accessed 1/18/2012 at http://www.nue-media.com/
Brand
Nuemedia's site is sleek, efficient, and professional. Use of the advanced grid makes a large amount of content available from the homepage with minimal effort, and the contrast of the bright images with the dark background makes them stand out.
Interactivity
A mobile "move to top of page" button stands out more than a static one would, making it easier to find and use. Other than that, interactivity is limited-- black and white to color fade is used, but more understated than it was on the SOFA page.
Functionality
The large amount of content fit into the grid makes for a very functional webpage, with blog posts, a twitter feed, and numerous portfolio pages all well-advertised.
Navigation
The advanced grid makes navigating to portfolio pages and other content easy, but the understatedness of the navigation bar and its failure to stay on the screen if you scroll below the fold could be seen as a navigation failure.

group94, full-screen photo, accessed 1/18/2012 at http://www.group94.com/#/website/work/portfolios
Brand
group94's full-screen photo layout uses a sort of half-tone dot pattern filter to evoke a very digital, technical, yet retro feel. The filter allows them to use any image in their portfolio, each image with its own theme, tone, and look, and unifies them all with a distinctive, branded look. Versatile and yet uniform at the same time.

Pikaboo, full-screen photo, accessed 1/18/2012 at http://www.pikaboo.be
Brand
Happy, exciting, active. The bright colors in all of the highlighted portfolios, as well as the photoshopped accents on the full-screen background photo, feel happy, and the motion-blur on the photographed model and sports photo in the first-displayed portfolio put forward a dynamic, active feel.
Interactivity
Moving underlines on the navigation bar are an interesting way to highlight selections.
Functionality
None of the portfolios would load until I updated QuickTime, which was unfortunate because most people hate dealing with stuff like QuickTime. The page also does not load correctly when zooming in and out, which is an issue that should be addressed.
Navigation
It wasn't until my second visit to the page that I realized you could scroll through the portfolio links in the center of the page. While there are many options to do so (mousewheel, narrow keys, on-screen arrow buttons), there could have been a better on-screen cue (larger or highlighted arrows, or some sort of autoscroll) to make it more clear. Navigating to find contact information or other information worked well.
